What Is Floriography?

The coded language of emotion — whispered in petals.

Floriography—the silent poetry of petals—spoke in blossoms when words fell short. Today we revive this timeless code as a modern design language: every bloom a symbol, every bouquet a message.

History of Floriography

Ancient Greece

In myths and poetry, flowers symbolized divine messages and eternal beauty.

17th Century

Ottoman tulips spoke of status and longing—elegant emblems of desire.

Victorian Era

In coded bouquets, emotions bloomed—each petal a secret message.

Present Day

Floriography returns in design, gifting, and emotional expression.

LOVE

The red rose stands as the eternal emblem of deep affection—its velvety crimson petals conveying passion, devotion, and the promise of unwavering heart.

"When words falter, let a rose speak."

LONGING

The forget-me-not’s tender blue bloom bridges distance and time, a whispered vow that “you remain in my thoughts,” no matter how far apart we wander.

"Even miles away, our hearts bloom together."

HOPE

The sunflower turns its golden face toward the light—an emblem of resilience, faith, and the promise of brighter days yet to come.

"In every sunrise, find the courage to bloom."

GRIEF

The white lily offers serene solace in times of sorrow, its pure petals embodying peace, remembrance, and the gentle acceptance of loss.

"In quiet reflection, memories blossom anew."

FORGIVENESS

The magnolia’s dignified bloom speaks of grace and renewal—the strength to let go, forgive, and open one’s heart to new beginnings.

"Release the past, and watch your spirit blossom."
